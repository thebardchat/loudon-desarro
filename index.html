The Facility

Built to Transform

A 200' × 250' Quonset hangar housing the world's first retractable concrete beam bleacher system. One building that becomes whatever the moment demands.

Competition Ready

NCAA Wrestling Arena

Three regulation 42'×42' competition mats. Space for simultaneous multi-mat tournaments. Full warm-up areas. Built to host regionals.

Patent-Worthy Innovation

PLEX FLEX™ System

16 rows of retractable concrete beams deploy 2,600 seats inside, outside, or split. Indoor arena to outdoor stadium in minutes. Nothing like it exists.

Multi-Sport

40-Yard Indoor Turf

Sprint training, agility drills, football combine prep, soccer practice. Marked yard lines. Climate-controlled year-round.

Elite Training

Strength & Conditioning

Power racks, Olympic platforms, cable machines, cardio. Glass walls overlooking the wrestling floor. Open to memberships.

Indoor/Outdoor

Glass Garage Doors

Full-height glass doors along the east wall open to an outdoor training field. Natural light, fresh air, maximum flexibility.

Community Hub

Grand Entrance & Amenities

Ticket sales, concessions, merchandise, locker rooms, offices, parent viewing areas, food truck parking. Built for game day.

The Legacy

Named for Legends

This complex carries the names of two coaches who shaped generations of wrestlers and young men across North Alabama.

In Memory

Coach Loudon

Head wrestling coach at Hazel Green High School. Taken too soon by a heart attack at the start of this season. Coach Loudon poured everything into his wrestlers — not just technique, but character. He believed every kid on that mat could become something. This complex exists because of what he started. His name goes on the front door.

In Honor

Coach DeSarro

Retired head wrestling coach at Grissom High School. One of the winningest coaches in Alabama wrestling history. Multiple state championships. Awards that fill walls. More importantly — he built men. He coached Coach Loudon. He coached me. The lineage runs deep. His name belongs on this complex because without him, none of us would be here.

The Vision

More Than a Building

I'm a dispatch operator. Father of five. Youth wrestling coach in Hazel Green. I've seen what a good coach can do for a kid — and I've seen what happens when there's nowhere for that kid to go train.

Birmingham has facilities. Nashville has facilities. But the kids between those cities? They drive hours or they don't go at all. That's the gap. And that's who this is for.

This complex isn't just for wrestlers. It's for the football player who needs a 40-yard turf in January. The parent who needs a safe place for their kid after school. The school that can't afford to build its own facility. The community that deserves something world-class.

Coach Loudon didn't get to see it finished. Coach DeSarro spent decades building something from nothing. This is what comes next.

“The kids between Birmingham and Nashville deserve a world-class facility. We're going to build it.”
Shane Brazelton · Founder
